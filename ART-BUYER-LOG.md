## Concept

An **Art Buyer** skill for creative directors and art directors working with AI tools. Replicates the role that existed at traditional ad agencies: when CDs/ADs have a concept, they brief the art buyer to conduct a search for emerging and established photographers. The art buyer has relationships with photographer reps and deep knowledge of current visual/photographic trends and traditions in art and photography.

**Why:** Stock photography is the enemy of distinctive creative work. The delta between "searched Getty" and "briefed an art buyer" is the difference between generic and authored. AI can replicate the art buyer's search capability — but only if it knows how to think like one.

**How to apply:** This skill would govern image sourcing across all MIJ and OTC projects. It would accept a creative brief (energy, territory, visual tradition, what to avoid) and return sourced options from non-stock channels.

## Source Channels (Non-Stock)

1. **Photographer portfolios** — online portfolios of emerging and established photographers, searchable by style/subject/tradition
2. **Film stills** — services like Genery for pulling frames from new and iconic films. Cinema is a rich, undertapped source of composed imagery.
3. **Fine art photography collections** — museum archives, gallery sites
4. **Photojournalism archives** — wire services, newspaper photo desks, documentary collections
5. **AI generation as last resort** — better to generate with AI than use stock, but human-shot is always preferred when available. Credit the human.

### Image Requirements

- All five receive identical post-production: B&W, midpoint ~38% luminosity, CSS `contrast(1.2) brightness(0.4)`, `opacity: 0.44`
- Must read as atmosphere behind 15vw Griffith Gothic Condensed — not foreground subjects
- No stock. No AI. Human-shot. Credit the photographer.
- Each image from a DIFFERENT visual tradition. The range is the argument.

### Art Direction Decisions

- **RAZZZMATAZZZ. — The zag:** The most famous Ali image, without Ali. Liston splayed on the canvas IS the evidence of craft. The viewer completes the picture.
- **FUNISSIMO. — Brightest of five:** LOOP rule: matched density = matched feeling, not matched histogram. The -issimo demands brightness; darkening destroys the faces and the ecstasy.
- **PANDEMONEYUM. — Getty comp:** Watermark managed via positioning. Full-res license ($375) deferred until gig lands.

### Observations for Skill Development
- The brief format (energy + visual tradition + search paths + what to avoid) is the reusable structure
- Coinage/wordplay analysis as a briefing input is unique to Omar's process but generalizable: any concept with language play should have the play decoded before the image search begins
- The "what to avoid" field is as important as the search paths — it prevents the art buyer from drifting into adjacent-but-wrong territory
- Provenance matters: an image discovered through real creative work (a pitch, a research phase) is stronger than one found by searching for the brief directly
- The LOOP validates final selections — the art buyer proposes, the creative team decides
- **The zag as art direction method:** When using an iconic image, consider removing its most famous element. The audience gap IS the art direction.
- **Brightness as argument:** Don't force uniform treatment across a set when one image's concept demands a different register.
- The LOOP validates final selections — the art buyer proposes, the creative team decides
